# Welcome to JavaScript Programming

You are about to learn how to make computers do things.

JavaScript is the programming language of the web. Every interactive website you visit — buttons that respond, forms that validate, menus that open — is powered by JavaScript. Learning it opens doors to web development, app building, and careers in technology.

This course is self-paced. You work at your own speed. Your instructor is here to support you — not to lecture to you. Read carefully, watch the videos, practice the quizzes, and ask questions when you are stuck. Keep in mind this is a new way of thinking. its normal to struggle a bit before it begins to click.

## How a Session Works

Each session has three parts. You complete them in order:

1. **Learning Page** — Read the content, watch the video, review the key concepts.
2. **Practice Quiz** — Short, ungraded self-check. Take it before you start the assignment.
3. **Assignment** — Hands-on coding task. This is where you earn points.

Each session should take a maximum of three hours. You may spread a session across more than one class period if you need to, but you are expected to complete one credit each week.

## Your Grade

- stats
- 500 | Major Projects (5 × 100 pts) | success
- 300 | Credit Quizzes (3 × 60 pts + midterm + final) | accent
- 125 | Session Assignments (25 × variable pts) | info

#### Your grade comes from three places:

**Session Assignments** — Every session has a hands-on coding assignment worth points. These are graded on a rubric and focus on code correctness, readability, and the stretch level you attempt.

**Credit Quizzes** — At the end of Credits 1, 2, and 4 there is a graded quiz worth 60 points. These draw from your session question banks.

**Midterm and Final** — The midterm (end of Credit 3) and final (end of Credit 5) are each worth 150 points and cover all sessions up to that point.

**Major Projects** — At the end of each credit there is a larger project worth 100 points. These ask you to bring together everything you learned in that credit.

## Six Parts of Every Learning Page

Every session page follows the same structure. Learn it once and you will always know where you are:

1. **Reading Assignment** — A section to review from the course materials.
2. **Video Tutorial** — A recorded demonstration on the LAN drive.
3. **Key Concepts Review** — Plain-language explanation you can refer to while working.
4. **Practice Quiz** — Directions to the self-check quiz in Canvas.
5. **Wrap-Up Summary** — What you learned and what comes next.
6. **Before You Move On** — A quick checklist to confirm you are ready to advance.

---

## Tips for Success

- **Read everything.** Instructions are in the page. Skipping them leads to confusion.
- **Watch the video.** The demonstration shows you how things work in real code.
- **Use the Key Concepts section.** It is your reference while you work on the assignment.
- **Take the practice quiz.** It shows you where your understanding is weak before you start coding.
- **Save often.** Use Ctrl+S (Windows) every few minutes. Unsaved work can disappear.
- **Ask questions.** Your instructor is in the room. Raising your hand is always the right move.

---

## Getting Help

> alert: info
>
> You will get stuck. That is normal and expected. Programming takes patience. When you are stuck: re-read the Key Concepts section, check your code for typos, and if you are still stuck after five minutes — ask your instructor.

Your instructor circulates the room and is available to help. You will never be bothering them by asking a question.
